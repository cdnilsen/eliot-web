1.1.μ [W]Unnanumunnaj {Lord} wutche ne ohunnag {Kesukod}!
1.1.E BLessed be the {Lord} for such a {Day!}

1.2.μ Kah {Lord} yeu nuttut wunnauumukqun, nishnoh noquttooweeksookeh noowehkummitchamun nanawehtouunnat ne {Kesukod}.
1.2.E And Blessed are we of the {Lord}, that once in every Week, we are called unto the Observation of the {Day}.

1.3.μ Quah Sohsummooe {Kesukod}!
1.3.E O Glorious {Day}!

1.4.μ uttiyeu en wame onkatogkek Kesudodash, woh yeu wuttune Sampoowoneau, {Kuttatupoat piogque Muttanonkannogdodtash nenauun!}
1.4.E To which all other Days may confess, {Thou art worth ten thousand of us!}

1.5.μ Kesukod, ne anooe pahkompommukquog nehtah wame Ohquompae nechenog: {Lord koowetomuk}; {Koonanimmitch kunnuke Kesukodash}.
1.5.E A {Day}, that is fairer than all the Children of Time: {The Lord is with thee; Blessed art thou among the Days.}

1.6.μ Ne {Kesukod} uttiyeu Kesukque {Kuhtahsoot wawekontog ukquhtiantamwehtouunnat}: kah ne adt pepenog woshwunumunnat Kesukque wunnunmpaukoonash wutche kuttummunke wunneechonoowooh woṣketompaog.
1.6.E The {Day}, which the {King of} Heaven {Delights to Honor}: and on which He chooses to open His Heavenly Treasures for the poor Children of men.

1.7.μ Kesukod ne pahke {Kesukquinneunkquohk}, kah {Wut-idchoowompioom}, Kesukque kah Mochemohtae Nooniuonkanun.
1.7.E The Day which is a very {map of Heaven}, and the {Day-break} of our Heavenly and Eternal Blessedness.

1.8.μ Kesukod uttiyeu ahhut Moocheke Nekhittit Wunnechonoh Mannit 
1.8.E The usual {Birthday} for the Sons of God.

1.9.μ {Kesukod} ne chappohtomukup na tanummunnehkehtauunnat {Peuhtamauwaonk} Nussonchumomun Jesus Christ yeu Muttaohket.
1.9.E It is a {Day} set apart for to maintain the {Religion} of our Lord JESUS CHRIST in the World.

1.10.μ Ne wunnetuppantamoe {Peuhtamooonk} wunnekin yeu Kesukod quhtiantosig qut nuppoomoo ne meshannantosig.
1.10.E That Holy {Religion} either Flourishes or Languishes, according to the Respect which this day meets withal.

1.11.μ Ne Annoohchamooonk Annoonehchomoouk yeu {Kesukod} annooe mishontammoomoo; 
1.11.E The {Commandment} which enjoyns the Observation of this {Day}, is more than ordinarily Expressive: 

2.1.μ kah aiyeuonk ne ahtunk ut nahshauwe nesunash nomatooe {Tableash} pahke nahtehchomoo ne nanawehtomik woh muhshompamukquet en nanawehtauunnat yeush naneswe.
2.1.E And the place where it stands between the {Two Tables} of the Law intimates an Extraordinary Influence, which the Keeping of it, would have upon our Keeping of {Both Tables}.

2.2.μ Maiatuh woh wunnanuhkosin annunummotamunnat {Peuhtammooonk} naose kekuttoohkame papame ukkesukodtum LORD, kunnikke yeug Lord ummissinninumog.
2.2.E It may be a Good Service unto {Religion}, to make the LORDS-DAY, the Subject of a brief Discourse among this People of the Lord.

2.3.μ Kah chaguas nashpe woh ne ohhunnag kekettoohkaonk {anooe} wunnuhteae kutchehtauun nehtah ne Ukkuttooonk God washottunk ne kesukod {howan} ootihe, kah ne uttiyeu.
2.3.E And with what can such a Discourse be better introduced, than that Oracle of God, which determines, {whose} the {Day} is, & {which} it is?

2.4.μ Yeu wuttissen Apostle {John} ne ummushamun {kesukod} ut {Weeksehchu}, {uttiyeu} adt {Wunnahshauanitoomoh God} ummishe {petuhshaonukup} kah wunnahtunukquannap, ne pish aniet um-Moeuwehkumonk Lord Jesus Christ, wutch ne ohquompiyeuut adt {Monchep} pache {Nahohtoeu uppeaonk.}
2.4.E Tis done by the Apostle {John}, declaring the {Day} of the {Week}, whereon the {Spirit of God} mightily took {Possession} of him, & gave him a Vision of what was to befall the Church of the Lord Jesus Christ, from the Time of His {going away}, to the Time of His {coming again.}

2.5.μ Matta wohtanoo wuttah noh annantog Apostle matta owwuttammoo {Chippeyeuwe Kesukod ut Weeksehchu} nashpe ne ohhuttag {Ukkesukodtum Lord}; Woh tahnooche yeu ukkod unnummussohhomun ne {Kesukod} adt monnaummansissah woh matta wohtauut.
2.5.E It is madness to think, That the Apostle does not mean a {Particular day of the Week}, by that which he calls, {The Lords Day}; there would not have been any USe, or any Sense, in going to tell the {day} of his Vision, with such a Term, if it must not have so been understood.

2.6.μ Nesunash muhshompamukquatte wunnomoonkash en wunneetuppantamwe nuppeuhtammooonkanun, yeuyeu nukqutehta pahke washaash.
2.6.E There are two points of much Improtance unto our Holy Religion, which now at once become Incontestable.

2.7.μ Nekone. Pahke unni, Pasuk Kesukod ut nishnoh Week woh unnussuppoawotomun ne chuppunnosooe ukkesukodtum LORD.
2.7.E First. It is plain, That there is {One Day in the Week}, that is, peculiarly to be acknowledged as, The LORDS-DAY.

2.8.μ {Kesukod} uttiyeu LORD {JESUS CHRIST} pehtuh nohnoine Wattihe, Kesukod uttiyeu LORD mahche nemunnuk, nashpe Mannitooe {Kuhquttummoonk}, wutche {nehenwonche} Wuttahtoonk; Kesukod uttiyeu woh wunneetuppantamwe nanawehtauog en Lordut; kah ne unne chuppunumog kah wunnetuppantamwehtauog en peuhtamwe pogkottant[a]mooonkash.
2.8.E A {Day}, wherein the LORD JESUS CHRIST has a peculiar Propriety; A {Day}, which the LORD has claimed by a Divine {Institution}, for {His Own}; A {Day}, which is to be observed by us, as {Holy} unto the LORD; and accordingly to be Dedicated and Consecrated unto {Religious Purposes}

2.9.μ Kah, Nahohtoeu; Pahke unni {Ukkesukodtum} LORD paiosuke ne {Kesudod ut Weeksehchu} ne yeuyeu Wanomuhtogeg Moeu nanawehtauhittit.
2.9.E And, Secondly. It is plain, That the LORDS-DAY, is that very {Day of the Week}, which is now generally observed among the Faithful.

2.10.μ Qut yeu toh unnoowasheau?
2.10.E But how does this appear?

3.1.μ Yeu wutchawasheau, Ne woh mo onkatog Kesukod, kah unnoowasheau nashpe {matta channantamwe Menaonchummooonk}; ne {pahke Menaonchummooonk} wahtauwohqueog yeush {Greeke Kuttooonkash}, Η{μὲρα κυριακὴ}, unneautammoomooash {Lord ukkesukodtum}.
3.1.E It appears from this, Because it can be No other Day. And it appears, from {Unquestionable Tradition}; the same {Unquestionable Tradition}, which assures us, that Η{μέρα κυριακὴ}, signifies {The Lords-Day}.

3.2.μ Nuttunukkuhque pahke waehtomun ne yeu adt huttamuk {ukkesudodtum Lord} nekone kesukod ut ooweesummoo Jewsog, ne woh ane pahke waehtomuk nishnoh onkatog yeu ohhunnag unnomai; woh mo Chaguas nuppahke waehtomun ne mahche pamshunk yeu matta pahke unnowaehtoooak.
3.2.E We have as much Assurance, That what is here called, {The Lords day}, was the {First day} of the {Jewish Week}, as we can of any such matter; we can be sure of {Nothing} that is past, if we are not sure of {This}.

3.3.μ Nuttannoowe pahke unnoowae[k]tomun, piosuke ne {Kesukod} yeu {Ussoowetamunnap} kah matta onkatog chuppe {ne}, ut wame negone Christiane Sohkuppomantamooonkash
3.3.E We do most certainly know, That this was the {Name} of that very {Day}, and of none by {That}, in all the first Ages of Chrsitianity.

3.4.μ Pahke nuttun waehtomun, {Clemens}, {Justin Martyr}, {Irenæus}, {Origen}, {Tartullian}, kah pasque waantamwe mukkinneonkash, kah wame Moeuwehkumonkane Wussuhkwhonganonchummooonkash, yeu wunnaquahchae unnoowohtamunneau.
3.4.E We are certain, That {Clemens}, that {Justin Martyr}, that {Irenæus}, that {Origin}, that {Tertullian}, that whole Councils, and all Church History, have all along so taken it.

3.5.μ Nux Nunnitchekonunnonut ahtaash OOsuhquahhonkash {Ignatius}, Wassuhquahhamukuppash, {John} wasuhquahhog {OOwoshwunnummooonk}, papaume {Swosuk} asuh Piogque kottummoo wuttuh issuh; kah yeush nuttukqunnonash ne kesukod ahhuttamuk, Ukkesukodtum LORD, ne Kesukod Nussonchumomun adt {Omohkep wutch nuppoonkanut}; 
3.5.E Yea, we have in our Hands the Composures of {Ignatius}, written within Eight or Ten years of {John's} writing the {Revelation}, which tell us, That the Day called, {The} LORDS DAY, was the Day of our {Lords Rising from the Dead}; 

3.6.μ kah nag matta kot Jewse-asechek, woh oonetuppantamwe nanawehtouunneau ne kesukod en Lordut; qut matta ne Kesukod yeu nakonuhshotamuk.
3.6.E and that they who would not Live in {Judaism}, should observe {this day}, and not the day before it, as Holy unto the Lord.

3.7.μ {Pliny} matta Peuhtamwe Nanawinuwaenin, ut OOsuhquahhunkanut en Kuhtahsoot {Trajan}, noh Kohtohsootummoowehpoh ne Apostle {John} wunnupun, unonchummoo, Christiansog pamontagpane ne ohquompiyeuut wuttahtouunnaous pasuk {Kuhquttosooe Kesukod} ne ohhut naquahchae moehtit wawenomonat CHRIST.
3.7.E {Pliny}, a Pagan Governour, in a Letter to {Tra[g]an}, the Emperour, in whose Reign the Apostle {John} died, reports, That the Christians then had a {stated day} in which they still met for the Praising of CHRIST.

3.8.μ Kah nuttunwaehtomun yeu {Paiosuke ne Kesukod}.
3.8.E And we all know, That it was {This very day.}

3.9.μ Newutche yeuyeu wane channoowae ushquhshatuh en nesunash matta channantamwe {pakodtuttumooonkash}.
3.9.E Let us then without any Disputation, pass on to two indisputable {Propositions}.

4.1.μ Nekone PAKODTUTTUMOOONK.
4.1.E The First PROPOSITION.

4.2.μ Ut {nukqutweeksooe Omppuntshomoonkanut} nukqutohteau {Kesukod} uttiyeu ummissinninumoh Manit woh nanawehtauhittit neane {Lord ukkesukodum}, kah neane {Sabbath} en {Lordut}.
4.2.E There is a {Day} in the {Weekly Revolution}, which is to be observed by the People of God, as the {Lords-Day}, and a {Sabbath} to the Lord.

4.3.μ Ut nishnoh weekse ohquompiyeuut nunnaquahchae quishke peaonukkumun, nukqut {Kesukquae ohquompi}, ne annuhtuhquahseog Winnetuppantamwe Nanawehtauunnate en Kehche ohquompiue Lordut
4.3.E In every {Week} of {Time}, there does revur one {Day} of {Time}, which we are bound for to keep {Holy} unto the Great {Lord of Time}.

4.4.μ Yeu wunnomuhtammooonk pish unnumminnehkehtosoo, woh mo wuttiṣkunumunnat nashpe yeu wutcheai.
4.4.E This Faith will be rendred Impregnable by this proof.

4.5.μ Waṣke kitchishik Muttaohk Kesukque God, chuppunnumup, pasuk {Kesukkod ut nesausuk tashukqunne ohquom[ip]yeuut}, woh winnetuppantamwe, nanawehtouunnat en wohhagkat.
4.5.E The God of Heaven, did at the very Beginning of the World, set apart a {Seventh Day of Time}, to be kept {Holy} unto Himself.

4.6.μ Mamaiattuh ne kuttooonk, {Mehquantash}, adt kitchishik yauwe nomatooonk noowekummukqun Ompammuhquaenat en ne adt wuṣke kuhquttumukup Sabbath, waṣke kitchishik Muttaahk.
4.6.E It may be, the word, {Remember}, at the Beginning of the Fourth Commandment, may invite us to look back unto the first Appointment of the {Sabbath}, at the Beginning of the World.

4.7.μ Newutche, Sun woh anooe pahkwomoo kuttooonk, nehtah ne aketomog, Gen. 2 2,3. {Nesausuk attashukqunnogok God uppakodchehtouunnap wuttannukosooonk ne mahche ayukup: kah anwosunup nesausuk attashukqunnogog wutch wame wutta[n]uhkosooonkannit ne kesehtunkup; Kah God OOnantamunnap nesausuk attashukqunnogok kah ne OOnetuppantamwehtouunnap}.
4.7.E For, Can any thing be more positive, than what we read? {Gen.} 2 2,3 {On the Seventh Day God Ended His Work which He had made, and He Rested on the Seventh Day, from all His Work which He had made; And God blessed the Seventh day, and Sanctified it}.

4.8.μ Chaguas mah {wuttanwosunnooonk God, nesausuk attushukqunnagok}?
4.8.E What was the {Resting of God} on the {Seventh day}?

4.9.μ Matta wutcheaiunnooup woh katinne Matta-Peuhtamweninuog yeu wuttinne Mamoshquohkontamunnaout ne unnuhquattummooonk; Mamoshquaohhahtit {Sabbath,} neane.--- {Delassati mollis Immago Dei}. Noochumwe wuttinsooonk Sauwunnummooe Mannit.
4.9.E There was no occasion for the old Heathen to scoff and jeer at the EXpression; flouting at the {Sabbath}, as,----- {Delassati mollis Imago Dei}.

4.10.μ Mat; God {wuttanwosun[n]ooonk} ne matta wepe unnautamun, {wuttahquehtoonk} ne {nukquttuh tashukqunne} mahche mah adt naquah[c]haanuhkosit, qut wonk {Wuttapinnauwatooonk}, ut ne wuttannuhkosooonkanut.
4.10.E No; The {Resting of God}, was His not only {Ceasing} from the {Work}, which He had for {six days} together been carrying on, but also taking {Satisfaction} in the {Work} which had been so far carried on.

4.11.μ Kah onk woh yeu mehquantamun, {God OOnantamunnap nesausuk attashukqunnogok}.
4.11.E For a Commemoration hereof, {God blessed the Seventh day,}

4.12.μ Yeu nautamun neane ut ohsuhkom[u]nooe kuttooonk; {Ne OOnetuppantamwehtouunnap}.
4.12.E That is, as it follows, {He Sanctified it.}

4.13.μ Kah, yeu toh woh unnautamun, chippe wuttannooohamooonk, woh ne noonetuppantamwe nanawehtouunnanonut en wohhogkat; Nuttannoonukqunnonup woh ne {noonetuppantamwehtouunnanonut}, asuh nutchuppu[n]numunnanonut, en wunnetuppantamoe auwohchaonkash. 
4.13.E And, what can this mean, but that He Enjoined the {Keeping} of it by us, as {Holy} unto Himself; He required, that we should {Sanctify} it, or Separate it for Sacred Uses.

5.1.μ Matta woh mussahhamun onkatog ahankonunkquoh unnautammooonk.
5.1.E It is impossible to give any other tolerable Interpretation.

5.2.μ Nux wunnekin qut Toh ohquompi nesausuk attashukqunnogok yeu unnoowunnetuppantamwehtouunnas?
5.2.E Well; But when was the {Seventh day} so {Sanctified}?

5.3.μ Yeu ussenap woṣketomp ne adt woh wuṣke kakenawe tapunnukup, wohwohtamehhonat, ne anakup.
5.3.E It was as Early as man could well be capable of being informed, that so it was.

5.4.μ Howan unnoowat God OOnetuppantamwehtoonk {nesausuk kesukod}, wutch waṣke kitchishik Muttaohk; [Heb 4 4.] wepe unnautamun ukquahshae kesantammooonk, pish pasuk peamese wuttohchimmoin ut {Judeae} ohkeut ne wunnanawehtouunneau, nese Muttanonk kah yauwe Paṣkooe kottummoo wuttuh issuh; woh wussome nanahtoenunkquet, kah koshki ne ohhunnag Nekonmushommooonk
5.4.E To say, That God {Sanctified the Seventh day} from the Beginning, even {from the Foundation of the World}; [Heb. 4. 4] only be determining that it should four and twenty Hundred years afterwards be kept by one Little People in the Land of {Judea}, methinks 'tis a very rude and harsh Anticipation.

5.5.μ Nahwutche wuttinnantamunneau annoohchamooonk woh wunnetuppantamwehtouunnat Sabbath wuṣke wunnunummoatinnap ut {Mara} tah tauwohkamukqut, qut nag kanhagk[o]g wutche yeu unnantammooonk chanuk unnomahshaog en ne at anooe kuppoooiak nehtah ne adt unnantamwehtit, Sabbath yeu tanukkitchishin.
5.5.E It is a Fancy of some, that the Command for an {Holy Sabbath}, was of no ancienter a Date, than the Station of {Israel}, at {Mara} in the Wilderness; But the Patrons of that Fancy do quickly run themselves into a greater Wilderness, than that wherein they suppose the {Sabbath} to have had its Original.

5.6.μ Nux {Moses} wahquttummonuhshont ummissinninumoh, Exod. 3. 1八. {Woh nag wuttonnoowout Touwohkamukqut Sephammaunônat Jehovah um-Mannittoomoowoah}, noh auwuttamup, woh nag wunnonche assoomanummooe nanawehtanunaout {Sabbath} en Lordut; newutche {Sabbath} ne mah {Sephosooe Kesukod}.
5.6.E Yea, when {Moses} demanded for his people, Exod. 3. 18. {That they might go into the Wilderness, to Sacrifice unto the Lord their God}; his Demand was, That they might go and keep an undisturbed {Sabbath} unto the Lord; For the {Sabbath} was the {Day of Sacrifices}.

5.7.μ Neane wame unnussumpoowaog, Jewsog nowat nanawehtopaneg wunnetuppantamoe Sabbath, nishnoh weekus; kah Jewse Sonchummoog unnussumpoowaog {Abraham} wonk wunnetupanatamwe Sabbath nanawehtooop; netatupe wutcheaiyeuoo unnantamunnat, OOnetuppantammoomoh God asquam Mashtammogkottunnook noh wuss[a]hsummoehhopaneh nashpe {wunnetuppantamwe Sabbath} nishnoh noquttooweeksookeh.
5.7.E As 'tis confessed by all, that an {Holy Sabbath} every Week was observed by the Jews of Old; and the Jewish Rabbins confess, That {Abraham} also kept the {Holy Sabbath}; So there is cause to think, that the Saints of God, before the {Flood}, Glorified Him with an {Holy Sabbath} every Week.

5.8.μ Kah ne annuhquattuk {Paræus}, {Sabbath wunnetuppantamwehtouunnap ut wutchashninneônkannoowout}, nekonne wuttoohshinoog.
5.8.E And that, as {Paræus} Expresses it, {The Sanctification of the Sabbath was observed in the Patriarchal Families}.

5.9.μ Nux nuttunaketosumun papaume {Kain} kah {Abel}, Gen. 4 3. {Ohquahshunk Kesukodash nag patooopaneg Ummakooonkannoo en Lordut}.
5.9.E Yeah, when we read of {Cain} and {Abel} Gen. 4. 3. {At the End of Days}, they brought their Offerings unto the Lord, 

5.10.μ Matta channantamenunkquet, yeu unnauuttamun nag nesh upattauunnaoh {Sabbath dayeu}.
5.10.E it means doubtless, That on the {Sabbath day} they brought their Offerings.

6.1.μ Kah pehpehtawe nanawompattamooonk {nesausuk attashukqunnogok, mashtommogohk}, monaog Kehche wahtoeninuog wuttunnantamhukqunneau, wonk Sabbath matta wutchukkuppohhomunnap {Arkut}, woh na matta tannunnanawehtouunnat.
6.1.E And the Repeated Observation of a {Seventh day}, in the Time of the {Flood}, hath made Learned men conjecture, That in the {Ark} too, the Keeping of an {Holy Sabbath}, was not shut out of Doors.

6.2.μ Nattootumwehchaonk.
6.2.E A Quæstion.

6.3.μ {Tohwutche kuhquttumunnas Sabbath?}
6.3.E {But why was the Sabbath Instituted?}

6.4.μ Ahque monuhtiekq kenau notieaque nah mussohhommauunog yeush Christiane unnantammooonkash.
6.4.E Let it not seem strange unto my Hearers, if I propose these {Christian Thoughts} unto them.

6.5.μ Nekone; Kohquttumuk wunnetuppantamwe Sabbath wutch waṣke kitchishik, ne ut wae nattonumop {Lord-Messiah} (asuch Christ) kah matta wepe {Kessehtoe} qut wonk {Mannoohwhae} annuhkosooonk mehquantamunnap ut ne kuhquttumooonkanehchu.
6.5.E First; In the Appointment of the {Holy Sabbath}, from the Beginning, there was a special Regard unto our {Lord-Messiah}; and not only the Work of {Creation}, but also the Work of {Redemption}, was regarded in the Appointment.

6.6.μ Nashpe muhsi wutcheae unnoowomoo, Mark 2. 2八. {Wosketomp wunnamonoh uppioquttumun Sabbath.}
6.6.E Most Reasonably it is said, {Mar.} 2. 28. {The Son of Man is Lord of the Sabbath.}

6.7.μ Sabbath pahke wuttauwamukqun wunnamonuoh woṣketomp; kah matta nutchannantammoo nussinnat, yeu aionioop nachah waṣke kitchishik.
6.7.E The {Sabbath} has a special Respect unto the {Son of Man}; and I make no stick to say, {It always} had so.

6.8.μ Nummoochukohtomun wutcheai unnantamunnat, ne {nakonohtogup Sabbath} kumma ohtop woṣketomp mahche {pinnishat}; {Nukquttuh attashukqunnogok} pehtuh ne Kesukod woṣketomp adt keshuttup, ne wuttut pinnishshonah.
6.8.E There is abundance of cause to think, That the {Fall} of Man was before the {First Sabbath}; on the {Sixth Day}, even on the Day whereon man was made, he {Fell}.

6.9.μ Unnoowomoo, Psal 49. 12. {Woṣketomp matta nukquttuttipohkahchoooup Ukquahtiantammooonkanut; Pinnishop aque mussunnohkontooonk neane puppunnahshimog neg auwohkohpunnacheg} ({asuh naputcheg})
6.9.E We are told, Psal. 49 12. {Man continued not a Night in his Excellence; He fell under Dominion, like the Beasts that perish}:

6.10.μ Asquam wanunkoounnookup, Mattannit noh wuttauwohkuhpunnae mussunnohkonop:
6.10.E Before {Night}, Satan obtained an horrible {Dominion} over him.

6.11.μ Qut paiomoouk negone Sabbath (asuh anwosunnooe Kesukod) ne wuttapehhukqunnah pinnishae woṣketomp (neane kehkinnasit, wunnekinnooe {Alting} noh waṣkinninue) nashpe ne mushkouantamonchummooonk papaume Mannoohoowaenin, uppohquahwunnukqunnat wutch wame yeush uhquanummooonkash uttiyeu {Kottun-ashkook}, ({Mattannit}) pahtunkup ut wohhogkat
6.11.E The Arrival of the {First Sabbath} comforted {Fallen Man}, (as the Excellent {Alting}, the younger, observes,) with the Joyful Tidings of a {Redeemer}, to Rescue him from the Confusions which the {Old Serpent} had brought upon him.

6.12.μ {Sabbath} wutche Kuhquttumunnap nohtuhkonat, Kessehtoonk toh inne wunnekin, kah wame Manehkesit toh inne tapinneuwatooup panuppehtunk wunnitchekane onuhkosooonkash.
6.12.E The {Sabbath} is to Celebrate the {Perfection} of the {Creation}, and the {Pleasure} which the Almighty took in perfecting the {Works} of His Hands.

6.13.μ Nux, qut wuttannuhkosooonkash God muttae mamoshkehtosoopash nashpe woṣketomp {ummatche usseonk}, ne chanuk noh adt ussep.
6.13.E Yea, but the {Works} of God, were wofully defaced by the {Sin} of man, presently committed upon them.

7.1.μ Yeush moohche {wiṣkehtauunash} kah {wussehtouunash} nashpe {Mannoohhoowae Nuppohquohsoowaeninumun}, nish panippehtouunnat; nish matta {panippehtosunnooash, Mannohhoowae nussonchummomun} toh Sahke matta annuhkosit nish wame wadchonumunnat wutch weṣke unni[u]onkanut, kah wuṣke aiumunnat nishnoh chag.
7.1.E There must be a {Renovation} and a {Restitution} of these {Works} by our Lord-Redeemer, for the {perfecting} of them; they are not {perfected} without and until our Lord-Redeemers undertaking to Save all from Desolation, and {make all things New}.

7.2.μ Yeu wutche {Sabbath} matta kuhquttumunnap wepe wawenotumunnat, ne annakuppash kessehtoe annuhkosooonkash asquam panshont woṣktomp, qut wonk wawenotumunnat ne momonantamwe quahshaweonk ne God aiukup, woṣketomp mahche matcheusset, mahquahtauunnat {kessehtoe annuhkosooonkash} wutch weṣke unniuonkanut, kah nesh tashunumunnat en anooe qunnuhque panippeuonkanehchu, nehtah nekonae, nashpe {Mannoohtooe Annuhkosooonkash}.
7.2.E Wherefore the {Sabbath} was to Celebrate, not only the State, wherein the {Works of Creation} were before the Fall of man, but also to Celebrate the Wonderful Provision which God made upon the Fall of man, to Rescue the {Works of Creation} from Ruine, and Raise them to an higher {perfection} than ever, in the {Works of Redemption}.

7.3.μ Nissim, Sabbath Kuhquttumunnap wutch waṣke Kitchishik, nohtukkonat kah wawenotumunnat ne annuhkosooonk uttiyeu {nussonchummomun Messiah} wunnoowae nemunnukup woh wuttissenat wutche Muttaahk.
7.3.E It was for a Celebration of what our {Lord-Messiah} has undertaken to do for the World, that an {Holy Sabbath} was at the Beginning Instituted.

7.4.μ Nahohtoeu; Wunnanummukqsooe God, OOche kuhquttumunnap wunnetuppantamwe {Sabbath}, wutch waṣke kitchishik Muttaahk, onk woh yeu wun-nashpe {kaken[o]wehhon} matcheseae woṣketompaog, wutche ne wunniuonk woh attumunumuk ut onkatog kah anooe wanekuk Muttaahket,
7.4.E Secondly; the {End} of the Blessed God, in Appointing an {Holy Sabbath}, from the Beginning of the World, was herewithal to {prepare} Fallen man, for the Blessedness of another and a better World.

7.5.μ nuttinne kunnoonitchamun, Heb. 4. 9 {Uhpomohteau Anwosunnooonk wutche Ummissinninumoh God.}
7.5.E We are advised, Heb. 4 9. {There doth Remain a Rest for the People of God.}

7.6.μ Wunnantooonkkanuoooo ne quahshawunummouut ummissinninumoh {Lord Messiah} wutch {waṣke kitchishik Muttaahk}.
7.6.E There is a {Blessedness} prepared for the people of the {Lord-Messiah} from the {Foundation of the World}.

7.7.μ Ne wunninuonk pish anwosunnooonkanuoooo wutch wame ne matta wanammattammooog.
7.7.E That {Blessedness} will be a {Rest} from all that is uneasy to us;

7.8.μ Anwosunnooonk wutch wame tahnoocheuonkash, kah moohmooshhittooonkash, {yeu matchetooe} Muttaohket; {anwosuunooonk} ut nummohtae ompuhkooonkanut God, noh pish wameyeuoouk nish$noh$ut en nohhoganonut.
7.8.E a {Rest} from all the vanities and vexations of this {present Evil World}; a {Rest} in the full Enjoyment of God, becoming {All} in {All} into us.

7.9.μ Kah yeu matta mahchiue unnantammooonk, unnantamunnat {Nesausuk Sohkohquompak, nesausuk Muttanonk} ut ne Sahkohtunk yeu Muttaahk, pish pasooche pattooomoo ne Mannittooe {Anwosunnooonk}.
7.9.E And it is no empty Notion, That the {Seventh period}, the {Seventh Chiliad} of the World, will bring on that {Rest} of God.

7.10.μ Yeuyeu, neane {wunnetuppant[a]mwe} Sabbath {K[o]hkinnasooonkanuook}, nahtuhkonat nattahtoonkanun ne {Michemohtae Sa[b]summooonk}, uttiyeu pish adt {wanotukkeg} Anwosunnehtit wutch wuttannuhkosooonkannoowout:
7.10.E Now, the {Holy Sabbath}, as 'tis a {Sign} of our Title to that {Eternal Sabbatism}, wherein the {Blessed} shall {Rest from their Labours}:

8.1.μ Nag ne nanawehtunkeg na {tannussealhommou[o]neau} matta wohkuhquohshinnooe wunniuonk ut Kesukque Muttaahket: Nemehkuh wonk, ne papane auwohchaonk woh nashpe kakenauwehchaog wutche {Wunniue nuttanwosunnooonkanun}.
8.1.E They who keep it, have the {Eternal Blessedness} of the Heavenly World therein {Seal'd} unto them: So, 'tis a notable {Mean} to fit us for our {Blessed Rest}.

8.2.μ Woṣktomp matta matche ussesoh nishnoh Kesukod Weeksehchu woh kakooche anogquinnunkquattuh Sabbath neanag; qut na[e]hoh Matcheusseog nummahche nashpe nuhkuhkunkumun monatash Muttaohke wuttammantommooonkash, muttae quenauwatta nutchuppunnummontchannanonut {Pasuk Kesukod ut nesausuk tahshinash}, ammanumunnat Muttaahke nottammantamooonkanunnonash, kah wepe woh nooweche ussoomomun God ut nesh usseonkash, woh quahshawehqueog wutche {Michemohtae noniuonkanun}.
8.2.E If man had not Sinn'd, Every Day of the Week, would have been more like a {Sabbath}; but since our Sin has plung'd us into so many Cares of this World, it is highly necessary for us to be accommodated with {One Day in Seven}, for to Lay aside our Worldly Cares, and be wholly taken up in Fellowship with God, and in those things, which are to fit us four our {Eternal Blessedness}.

8.3.μ Nahohtoeue PAKOTTUTTUMMOOONK.
8.3.E The Second PROPOSITION.

8.4.μ Tapinnunkquet woh mussikkenooe God ukkuhquttumun ne Kesukod ut Weeksooe {Uhhopuntshomooonkanut} uttiyeu nakum annantog woh nanawehtauun, neane nootetuppantamwe Sabbatoomun; kah yeuyeu ukkuhquttumun ne {Kesukod} ut Weeksehchu, Nussanchummomun adt Omohkep wutch nuppooonkanut.
8.4.E 'Tis fit that the Great God should Appoint, {which day} in the {Weekly Revolution} He will have to be observed as our {Holy Sabbath}; and He does now Appoint the {Day} of the Week, whereon our {Lord Rose} from the Dead.

8.5.μ Yauwe nomatooonk, wepe weogquttummoomoo {Pasuk Kesukod ut nesausuk tahshunash} woh nawehtomuk neane Sabbath-day.
8.5.E Our {Fourth Commandment} speaks only of {One day in seven}, to be kept as a {Sabbath day}.

8.6.μ Kah wunnetuppantamwe Wun-Nashauanittoomoh God, mahche Sohchukquanumoh ummissinninnumoh God, {Chague Kesukod}, ut weeksehchu ummahche pepenamun, kah kuhquttumun.
8.6.E And the Holy {Spirit of God} hath signified unto the People of God, {which day of the seven}, He hath pitched upon.

8.7.μ Aque nekone Testamentut, {nesausuk attashukqunnogok} wutch {aiummooonkanut Muttaahk Sabbattooomooup}. 
8.7.E That which was the {Sabbath-day} under the {Old Testament}, was the same that has been thought generally to have been the {Seventh-day} from the {Creation}.

8.8.μ Kah pahke unni, ne {K[a]sukok} nahohtoeu anohtog, yeuyeu nuttohtouunnan, nashpe wussumpohshahsoowaonk Wunnetuppantamwe Nashauanit, wutche {Wunnetuppantamwe Nus-Sabbattoomun}; nuttauwuttam ne Kesukod nul-Lordoomun adt {Omohkep wutch nuppooonkanit}. 
8.8.E And 'tis clear, that the {Day} following it, is by the Direction of the Holy Spirit, now become our {Holy Sabbath}; even the Day of our {Lords Resurrection from the Dead}.

8.9.μ Nussonchimmomun adt Sepsuk oowenohkeut mamusse {Katinneue Sabbath}, NE noh wuttut nuhkohsooe asuh poosuhkinsooe nukkotumunnap. 
8.9.E Our Lord Lying in His Grave, all the {Old Sabbath}, has left that {Old Sabbath} buried there.

8.10.μ Kah ne Oswohtouun {Sabbath} wutch {uhquae Kesukod} en {negone Kesukodut} ut Weeksehtchu, nahwutche Mannittooompaog noh wuttut kehkinnasinneau kah mussohhomunneau wahweke tapinnunkquet. 
8.10.E And some have remarked a curious Agreeableness, in the Translation of the {Sabbath} from the {Last day} of the Week, to the {First}.

9.1.μ {Jewsooe Sabbath} nakose unni ne annakup nekone {Wunnoowaonk}; nekone {annuhkosooe Kesukodash}, neit quahshotooonk woh Anwosununnat, kah pomantamunnat. 
9.1.E The {Jewish Sabbath}, had a little strain of the {Old Covenant} in it; First the {Working days}, and then the Promise of {Rest} and {Life}.

9.2.μ Qut {Christiane Sabbath} anooe unnogquinnunkquet ne anag {Wuske wunnoowaonk}; ne nekone anummonkqut wunnomuhtamenin {wunnahchae} kah {anwosunnooe} quahshotooonk asquam aset {Sampiue annuhkosooonkash}. 
9.2.E But the {Christian Sabbath} has a greater strain of the {New-Covenant} in it; which first gives the Believer the Promise of {Rest} and {Peace}; before he does the {Works of Righteousness}.

9.3.μ Yeu pahke unni; Matta noquattinnoo onkatog Kesukod ohunnukuhque tapinnunkquoh wutche {Wunnetuppantamwe nus-Sabbattoomun}, neane NE Nussonchimmomun noh mannoohqueogup adt pakotchehtunkup {Mannoohoowae Wuttannuhkosooonk}, kah mushkauantamwe Sohkommonuhshukqueog wame nummatwomunnonog, kah nishnoh chag aiyeuohkontogup noowadchanchaonkanun. 
9.3.E This is very sure; There appears no Day so suitable for our {Holy Sabbath}, as the Day whereon our Lord-Redeemer, {Finishing} His Work of Redemption {Triumphed} over the Enemies and Hindrances of our Salvation.

9.4.μ Unnannoonittin (newutche ne woh unnukqushkunumun) Isai. 5八 13. {Pish kuttuhtamun Sabbath wekontanmooonk}: (ne unnautamun pish kunnanawehtouun neane wunnetuppantamwe wekantamoe Kesukod) en {OOnetuppantamoomoh Lord, noh Sohsumooe} (asuh Sohkosooe) {howannooonk}. 
9.4.E It is required, (for so 'tis to be rendered,) Isa. 58. 13. {Thou shalt call the Sabbath a Delight}, (i.e. Keep it as a Day of Holy Delight,) {unto the Holy one of the Lord, the Glorified} (or Victorious) {One}.

9.5.μ Noh nul-Lordoomun Jesus Christ ohhunut, {OOnetuppantammoomoh God}; 
9.5.E It is our Lord Jesus Christ, who is, {The Holy One of God}.

9.6.μ Kah ut {Omohkeae ukkesukodumut} yeu inne {Sohsummoowehhopoh}, kah ne wuttut {Sohkosooweninuinnap}. 
9.6.E It was on the {Day} of his {Resurrection}, that our Lord was {Glorified} and Victorious.

9.7.μ Sabbath unnukkesukodtooooo woh na nuttut {Wekontamunnan} ut noh {Wanetuppantog}, (nooweachum Christ) kah ut {Wussohkosooonkash}; newutche mata woh nuttohtomun anooe tahapinnunkquohk Kesukod wutche nus-Sabbattoomun nehtah {Sohkosooe Ukkesukodum}. 
9.7.E The {Sabbath} being a Day for us to {Delight} in the {Holy One}, and in his {Victories}, what {Fitter day} can we have to be our {Sabbath-Day}, than the {Day of His Victories}?

9.8.μ Ohtauogus piahchummooonk pepenamunnat, Sun woh nuppepenamunnonup anooe tahtapinnunkquoh Kesukod, nehtah ne yeu ane {Mishshehtosik}? 
9.8.E Had we been left unto our own Conduct, what fitter Day could we have chosen for our {Sabbath}, than a Day so Ennobled?

9.9.μ {Samuel} yeu OOcheakohkonap wutche pepenauaonk {Saul} [1 Sam. 10. 24.  {Matta howan wuttunauuh}, anooukqunnuhquissoomoh wame nag asqueuwitchek missinninuog. 
9.9.E {Samuel} argued for {Souls} being chosen, because there was {none like him}, a man higher than the rest of the People.

9.10.μ Wunnummuhkit yeu Kesukod mishshe tahshunnosoo nashpe nussonchimmomun Wuttomohkeonk naut, kah anooe qunnuhquehtosoo nehtah nanwe nish nesausuk attahshehkish, Toh wutche woh matta nissimun, {Chuh, namuk ne Kesukod uttiyeu Lord pepenogup}!
9.10.E Truly, This Day is advanced by our Lords {Resurrection} upon it, and made much Taller than any of the Seven, Why should we not say, {Behold, the Day which the Lord has chosen}!

10.1.μ Newutche quaquahshae anoowonap ut nekone {Testamentut}, pish {Wunnetuppantamwe Sabbattoomoo}, nussonchimomun Omohkeae {Ukkesukodum}, wutche ummissinninumoh. 
10.1.E It was therefore foretold in the {Old-Testament}, That the Day of our {Lords Resurrection} would be the {Holy Sabbath} of His People.

10.2.μ Nuttunaketomumun. Psal. 110. 3. {Kummissinnonumog pish nanauwe} (asuh Wekontamwe) {Makooweninuog, ne Kesukod minnehkesean}. 
10.2.E We read, Psal. 110 3 {Thy People shall} be a People of {Free-will-Offerings, in the Day of thy Power}.

10.3.μ Nussonchimmomun omuhkeae ukkesukodum, wutch Nuppooonkanut, ne pehtuh nohnoine ummunnehkesooe Kesukodum: {Nashpe munnehk[e]soonk unnoowashannopoh noh wunnamonuh God, nashpe Omohkeonk wutch nuppooonkanut.} 
10.3.E The Day of our {Lords Resurrection} from the Dead, was eminently the {Day of His Power}: He was, {Declared the Son of God with Power}, by the {Resurrection from the Dead}.

10.4.μ Winnekin, kah ummisinninumoh woh yeu Kesukodut up-Patauunneau nanauwe ummakoonkannoowoash. ---Weche natwontash, {Psal}. 11八. 24. Kah neit ne mishe quhtianittooonk chachinne poonumuk ut {Swosuk Kesukodut}, (ne kesukod ohsuhkommoomoouk, ne nesausuk ottahshuhk) Ogquinnunkquet kanummoomoouk, quahshotummooonk papaume {Christiane} nus-Sabbittoomun. 
10.4.E Well, and this is to be the Day for His People to bring their {Free-will-Offerings} unto Him.----Compare, Psal. 118. 24.

10.5.μ Qut {Sabbath ne ukkesukodtum Lord}! 
10.5.E And the Singular Honour then sometimes put upon the {Eighth Day}, (the Day after the {Seventh}, seems to have carried in it, a prediction of our {Christian Sabbath}.

10.6.μ Kah matta chaguas wonk quenauwat, yeu woh patooomoo michemontae {Auwosunnooonk} en {Sabbattaeninue pussomat[o]oonk}. 
10.6.E But that which may procure an Everlasting {Rest} unto the {Sabbatarian Controversy}, is this; and there needs no more: 'Tis the {Lords-Day!}

10.7.μ Yeu wamat wunnomoehtounat, nashpe kesque Kuhquttummooonk, ne woh {wunnetuppantamwe} nanawehtouun en Lordut. 
10.7.E That's enough to prove, that is by the Institution of Heaven, to be kept {Holy} unto the LORD.

10.8.μ Onuhque woh nissimun; Ne Kesukod uttiyeu nashpe naneswe {Wuttannoochamooonkannoo}, kah {wuttashoowaonkannoo} Winnetuppantamwe Apostlesog waenotosik en nohhoganonut wutche {Winnetuppantamwe} nus-Sabbattoomun; ne Sabbattooomoo nashpe wuttannohchamooonk God nehenwonche. 
10.8.E We may add; The {Day} which both the {Præcept} and the {Pattern} of the Holy Apostles, have commended unto us for our {Holy Sabbath}, is the Day for it Commanded by our Lord Himself.

10.9.μ Qut yeu unni ne kesukod yeuyeu nanawehtouog neane UKKESUKODUM LORD. 
10.9.E But such is the Day, which we now observe as the LORDS DAY.

10.10.μ {Baronius} nashpe papane wutcheai unnoowau, “UKKESUKODUM LORD nanawehtouunnap ut Apostlesog wuttohquompiyeumoowout newutche wame nag waohtunke wuttohhoowooh kuhta pish unnoowaog, matta howan ne ukkuhquttumunnah chippe nag nehenwonche.” 
10.10.E {Baronius} with very good Reason sayes, "That all who are in their Witts, will say, that since it is found, that the LORDS DAY was observed in the Apostles Times, it could not be ordained and Commanded to be kept of any other but of themselves."

10.11.μ Kah koowahtomoo chaguas ohsuhkommoomoo! 
10.11.E And you know the Consequence!

10.12.μ OOne unnoowussuttumun {Tertullian}; “Apostlesog matta chaguas pepenampane uppiahchummooe pethunumunaout, qut wunnomoe kuhquttummouopane wuttohchummoinnash  nish attummunummehtip wutch Christut.”
10.12.E 'Tis well judged by {Tertullian}; "The Apostles chose nothing which they brought in at their own pleasure, but faithfully appointed unto the Nations the Discipline which they received from Christ."

11.1.μ Wunnomoonk wame Sabbath dawe unniyeuonkash Osoowohtouunnapash en yeu nukkesukodumunnanut, nashpe wuttomohkeonk nussonchummomun Christ: qut yeu tokonogque ogquinnunkquet wanomuhtogkeg ko ummoeu kah panippe yeu nanawehtouunnaoh papeomooukup ne kesukod ahtam[u]k {Pentecost}, ne matta anooe chanuk paomooukup nehtah {nesausuk tashe nesausuk} Kesukodash ne ohsuhkoe. 
11.1.E Indeed, tho' the {Resurrection of our Lord} had Consigned over unto this Day of ours, all the Rights of the {Holy Sabbath}, yet it is probable, the Day was not compleatly advanced into the practise of the Faithful, until the Day of {Pentecost}, which was not until {Seven times seven} Days after it.

11.2.μ Wunnomoonk nussonchummomun Jesus Christ, yeu Kesukod wunnuhnompe mishe quhtiantamwehtouunnap, nashpe uppepenammooonk na wuttut nahtunnonat wohhoguh en ukkod-Nuhtuhtoueninumoh. 
11.2.E Our Lord Jesus Christ had, its true, several Times put a singular Honour on this Day, by His chusing to Exhibit Himself unto his Disciples upon it.

11.3.μ Qut nussonchummomun Jesus Christ mahche tashanut ut {Wuttappooonkanut Wuttinohkonit God} Wussookunumunnap {OOnetuppantamwe Nashauannittoom} ut OOt-Apostlesumog. 
11.3.E But when the Advancement of our Lord Jesus Christ was Consummated, in {Sitting at the Right hand of God}, He poured out His {Holy Spirit} on His Apostles.

11.4.μ Ne Sookunummooonk {Wunnetuppantamwe Nashauannit} nahtunnukhittip mamusse wuttunnantammooonk God, mah paioske ut ne kesukodut. 
11.4.E That pouring out of the {Holy Spirit}, which Revealed the whole will of God unto them, was on {that very day}.

11.5.μ Kah nawowutchee nunnamehhomun Apostlesog wunnaquahchae chuppeyeue uss[e]htamuneau ne kesukod neane {Wunnetuppantamwe} en Lordut. 
11.5.E And from {that very day}, we find the Apostles distinguishing the {Day} as {Holy} unto the Lord.

11.6.μ Ne wutchuppe poonumunnaoh nashpe {wuttannoohchamooonkannoo}, 1 Cor. 16. 2.  {Nekone Kesukok ut Weeksehchu nishnoh pasuk kenau chuppe pooni[o]h monummooonkanit}. 
11.6.E They distinguish'd it by their {Precept}: 1 Cor 16. 2. {Upon the First Day of the Week, Let every one of you lay by him in store.}

11.7.μ Tohwutche annooantamun nekone Kesukod ut Weekehchu nehtah onkatogane Kesukodash wanomuhtogkeg woh wuttut moenaout wauwussummonat God? 
11.7.E Why should the {First day of the week} be preferr'd before any other Day, for the Faithful to Assemble unto the {Service of the Lord}:

11.8.μ Matta woh mussohhomun onkatog wutcheai chippe yeu, Ne {Wus-Sabbattoom Lord}. 
11.8.E There can be no Reason given, but because it is the {Sabbath of the Lord}.

11.9.μ Wutchuppe poonumunnaoh wonk nashpe wuttoshoowaonkannoo: Acts 20. 7. {Nekone Kesukod Weekusut Kodnuhtuhtoneninuog mooopane, Kah Paul Ukkuhkootumauuh}. 
11.9.E They distinguish'd it by their {Pattern}: Acts 20. 7. {Upon the First day of the week, the Disciples came together and} Paul {Preached unto them}.

11.10.μ Tohwutche ut {nesausuk} Kesukodash (pakuh na ne wuss[o]hkohkonah {Paul}) Kodnuhtuhtoueninuog wepe wuttut moenaoh {nekone Kesukod Weeksehchu}. 
11.10.E Why should the {First day of the Week} be the only Day in {Seven} (for so long had {Paul} been there,) for the Disciples to {Come together}?

11.11.μ Matta woh mussohhomun ankatog wutcheai chuppe yeu, Ne Kesukod mahche nag wus-Sabbattoomoo ta[h] ne ohquompiyeuut. 
11.11.E There can be no Reason given, but because it was become the {Sabbath} unto them.

11.12.μ Nuttiohqueim, Noonhoosumun nashpe wussumpoowaonkannoo nag annooe minnehke aiyeuohkontogeg Sabbath tah yeu Mutaohket, Unnoowaog, “Nanawehtoonk {Ukkesukodum Lord}, attumunumunnap nashpe ummoae tapinnauwatooonkannoo wame Moeuwehkumonkash yeu Muttaohket, nux wutch kah ut Ukkesukodumoowout Apostlesog, kah nashpe nag nehenwonche ukkuhquttumooonkannoo.”
11.12.E In short, We are arm'd with Confessions from the greatest Adversaries of the {Sabbath}, "That the Observation of the {Lords Day}, was received by the common consent of all the Churches in the World, even from and in the Apostles Days, and this with the Approbation and Authority of the Apostles themselves."

12.1.μ Qut Apostlesog matta chaguas kuhquttummauwaog Moeuwehkumonkash chuppe nashpe wussumpahshahsoowaonk Wunnetuppantamwe Nashauanit. 
12.1.E Now the Apostles directed the Churches to nothing, but what they had a Commission from the Lord Jesus Christ, and a Direction from His Holy Spirit for.

12.2.μ Momachisheue; {Jewsoowe Sabbath} mamahche ammanumukus, onkatog {Kesukod} neit moohche nanawehtauun neane Christiane {Sabbath}. 
12.2.E In fine; If the {Jewish Sabbath} be abrogated, then some {other day} must be kept, as the {Christian Sabbath}.

12.3.μ Qut matta onkatog Kesukod unnoowomunnoo yeuuh neen, chuppe ne Kesukod yeuyeu nanawehtauwehhittit wanumohtogkeg. 
12.3.E But no {other day} pretends to be so, besides the Day now kept among the Faithful.

12.4.μ Jewsooe Sabbath pahke ammanumun, asuh kohkonnehtouun, Col. 2. 16. {Ahque howan wussumukkook, wutche} (asuh papaume) {ne Sabbath}. 
12.4.E The Abrogation of the {Jewish Sabbath}, is very peremptory; Col. 2 16. {Let no man Judge you, in respect of the Sabbath-days.}

12.5.μ Nish {Sabbath-dayash} nanawehtomukuppash, nishnoh noquttooweeksookeh kunukke Jewsog moohche auwuttosooash toanoo unnoowomoo, {Ahque howan wussumukkook papaume nesh}; kah yeu unnauuttanun ahque howae wosktomp na kuttut mussunnohkonukqunneau, asuh koosummukkoo, nesh matta nanawehtoooog.
12.5.E The {Weekly Sabbath-days} observed among the Jews, must be the only Things intended, when it is said. {Let no man judge you in respect of them}; That is, let no man go to Impose them upon you, or {Judge} that you Sin, if you do not keep them.

12.6.μ {Sabbattooo}, ne woh nanawehtomukup {Jewsooe Sabbath}, woh matta yeuyeu nanawehtouun; Tohkuh momachish unni Christiane nus-Sabbattoomun yeyeu peomoo ne {Sabbath} ne ahtogup. 
12.6.E Some {Sabbath} must be kept; The {Jewish Sabbath} mayn't be kept; It remains, that our {Christian Sabbath} take the place of it.

12.7.μ MUHSOGQUNUMMOOONK asuh, AUWOHCHAONK. 
12.7.E APPLICATION.

34.7.E The Reformed Churches abroad have commonly indulged themselves, in loose {Principles}, and loose {practices}, about the {Lords-Day}; as if the Necessity of keeping the {Lords-Day}, were, {Figmentum Anglicanum}; An English Fancy!

34.8.μ Qut wunnummuhkit, yeu matta wepe {unnantammooonk} Peuhtomooonk mut[t]ae Ishkotpaket ut nesh Moeuwehwehkumonkash, kah uhquinnunkquate Paquanuonkash, nag wunnaquahchae kah ohhohsuhkoe auwohkuhpunnanukqunneau.
34.8.E But surely, tis no Fancy that {Christianity} runs at a low Ebb, in those Churches, and that horrendous Desolations are continually and successively harassing of them.

34.9.μ Napehnont woh nag momachish nannompah nukquit nattootumwehchahtit, Sun ummatta Uppoohpoohqunnumooonkanoo God {Wuttanwosunnooonk} ne kehche wutcheai wache God nashpe uhquinnunkquate Unninneaonkash kah Mishehtashinash nanuk poohpooqunum {Wuntanwosunnooonkanoo}. 
34.9.E Oh! That they would once at last Enquire, Whether their breaking of {Gods Rest}, be not a grand Reason why God with such plagues and storms, has been breaking of {Theirs.}

34.10.μ {Englishog} anooe wawuntash Wus-Sabbattoomoooash nehtah nanne onkatogane Wuttohchummonash chippe pasuk ut ne Munnanahhannut. 
34.10.E The {English Nation} has better {Sababths} than any in the World, except another Nation on the same Island with it.

35.1.μ Qut nukquit na$ut pohquae aiyeuohkontamunnappash Wus-Sabbattoomash God, kah ne chanuk pattooomooup uhquinnunkquate Mishehtashin ut wohhogaout nag ne ayumwehhitip, {kah anooe muhsi moshquantooonk}. 
35.1.E But an open violence there once offered unto the {Sabbaths} of God, quickly issued in an {Horrible Tempest} on the Authors of it, and a {Wrath unto the uttermost}.

35.2.μ Nunnunukishem aketamunnat nesh kuttooonkash, Jer. 17. 27. {Matta wunetupantamwehtoooog  Sabbath day, pish nunnashqunnanum Mohchikkissumooe Noohteau, ne matta pish ohthommoomuk}. 
35.2.E I tremble when I read those words; Jer. 17.27. {If you will not Sanctify the Sabbath-Day, I will kindle a Fire that shall Devour, and shall not be quenched.}

35.3.μ OOwe, Noowammontamwe Bostonoom, {Sun ummatta kukquihtamoo mahchikkissumooe Noohteau}? 
35.3.E Ah, my dear {Boston}, Art thou not afraid of a {Devouring Fire}?

35.4.μ Napehnont woh Kus-Sabbattoomash Kehkinnasooonkash wutche koowatchanchaonk!
35.4.E Oh! That thy {Sabbaths} may carry the Symptoms of thy preservation!

35.5.μ Qut Pahke waehtoook {howanooe} nux kah michemohtae uhquanummooonkash, nesh pish Wesoganue kah Kehkishquogate ummechumooonkash yeu Matcheseonk
35.5.E But be assured, That {personal plagues} also, will be the bitter and bloody Fruits of this Impiety. {Personal plagues}, yea, {Eternal} ones.

35.7.μ Nuttunaketosumun papaume nag auwohkuhpunnachig, Rev. 14. 11. {Wuttauwohkuhpunae Qussohtomoo Oushpemoo micheme kah micheme, kah nag matta anwosunnooog Kesukodae asuch Nuhkonnae}. 
35.7.E We read of them that perish, Rev. 14. 11. {The Smoke of their Torments ascended up for ever and ever, and they have no Rest Day nor Night.}

35.8.μ Woi ahque tummuhhoowash {Uhquammammooonk woh mo adt anwosunumuk}, nashpe kussasekinnamooonk Sabbathe {Anwosunooonk}.
35.8.E Oh, don't incur a {Torment without Rest}, by thy Antipathy to a {Sabbath of Rest}.

35.9.μ {Chepiohkamukqut} matta ohtanoo {Sabbath}; (neanag wuttunquomooonkanoo ahsooke Jewsog) Toh inne tapinnunkquet yeu Matchetooonk wutche wame nag Sikinnakig Sabbath yeu ut Muttaohket! 
35.9.E There is {no Sabbath in Hell}; (as the fond Jews have dreamed) How properly does the Wretchedness belong to them, that would have {no Sabbath on Earth}!

35.10.μ Pasuk wosketomp nootog munnehke wehquttooonk, woh wunetuppantamwehtouunnat Sabbath, yeu wuttunnoowahhomunnah, Sun Kukkodantam wosketomp wunnaquahchaauwohkuhpunnonat quinne Kesukod!
35.10.E One hearing the {Sanctification of the Lords-Day} urged for, cryed out, {What, would you have a man be upon the Rack a whole Day together!}

35.11.μ Woṣ[k]etomp, Auwohkuhpunnanushk {Ukkesukodum Lord}, tatuh woh anammattaman Kesuk, ne pish {Michemohtae Sabbattoomoouk}! 
35.11.E Man, if the {Lords-Day} put thee upon the {Rack}, I wonder how {Heaven} will agree with thee; {Heaven} which will be an {Everlasting Sabbath!}

35.12.μ Nag eyeuyeu annoowachig papaume Sabbath, {Ne toh inne Sauanummukquat}?
35.12.E Such as now say of the {Sabbath}, {what a weariness is it?}

35.13.μ Wunnummuhkit matta quenauattunnoo nissinnat, nag woh {Sauunummehtoog} Kesuk; nuttanoowe pepenam nissinnat, nag woh ne {Wussechako[n]konoo}, woh unnag na uppetshonoowout, nag neane neṣkinnunkquissitcheg! 
35.13.E certainly, I need not say, {Heaven} would be {weary} of them; I will rather say, they would be {weary} of {Heaven}, if it were possible for those {Defilers} to {Enter} into it!

35.14.μ God mahche quahshawunum ne pish attanauwohkuhpunnanut yeu ahhansitcheg Matcheseaeninuog, Tannauohkuhpunnaonk uttiyeu pish apehtamwehtit, matta wepe pasuk quinne kesukod, qut Piogque Paṣkooe Muttannunk tashe Muttannunkanne Sohkuppomantamooonkash, nux {Michemeyeue, Michemeyeue, Michemeyeue}!
35.14.E God has provided a {Rack} for such Criminals; but a {Rack}, on which they are to Ly, not for {a whole Day together}, but for ten Million Ages; for {Eternity, Eternity, Eternity!}

36.1.μ Woi! {Yeu natwontomook kenau wannantamaque} LORD UKKESUKODUM {ishkont kussukqshannukkoo, kah matta howan wuttapin woh pohquohwunnukqueog}.
36.1.E Oh! {Consider this, ye that forget the} LORDS DAY, {lest he tear you in} Pieces, {and there be none to deliver you}.

36.2.μ Nuppokodoppoam nashpe Ukkuttooonkash Pasuk wawenotosooe, {Parisiane} Ummukkunneonkanoo Waaontogig, kah nuttunwaehto wame manunniue Christiansog, ut yeu mukkinneonkanehchu, pish OOwaminamunneau ne Pakodtuttummooonk; 
36.2.E I conclude, with the words of a famous {Parisian} Council; and all the Serious Christians in the Assembly, will I know, Agree in this Conclusion; 

36.3.μ {Tapin[e]unkquat wutche wame nag Mannoohwhitcheg nashpe ummonanchaonk Christ, wuttahquenaout ut ne Kesukodut Pomantamehhoowaenin adt Omohkep wutch nuppooonkanut, kah nag adt unuunumauuttup, Annoosooonk, woh wuttomohkenaout wutch yeu Muttaohke wekontammooonkash, kah Nehhenwoncheyeue; kah woh numwamechummooog wepe nashpe Nashauannittooe Wekontammooonkash kah wawuttooe ohsoomap[o]oog, nashpe mamusse wuttohhoowoash ut matta anwosunooe wawwenomaonkash Lord}. 
36.3.E {It is fit for those that are Redeemed by the Grace of Christ, to abstain on that Day whereon the Author of Life Rose again, amd gave them Hope of a Resurrection, from the Worlds pleasures and their own; and be filled only with Spiritual Joyes, and busily Vacant with all their Hearts in the restless Praises of the Lord.}

36.4.μ UNNEH.
36.4.E FINIS.
